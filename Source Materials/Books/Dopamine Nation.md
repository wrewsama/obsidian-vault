## pursuit of pleasure
- we're constantly consuming and reacting to stimuli - especially from our devices
- boredom creates the space necessary for new thoughts to form
- the brain develops a tolerance to higher levels of dopamine, making things less pleasurable. But if after a sufficient waiting period, the brain returns to baseline

## self-binding
- self-binding: the intentional creation of barriers to block us from the things we're addicted to
- this can be physical (making the thing inaccessible), chronological (time-limiting it), or categorical (sorting dopamine into what we allow and what we don't allow)

## pursuit of pain
- the brain maintains homeostasis between pleasure and pain in both ways: pleasure is followed by pain, pain is followed by pleasure, and both effects shoot past the baseline
- it is still possible to overconsume, or even get addicted to, pain
- social media lets us create "false self" to protect us against the demands of the world, leading to feelings of profound emptiness
- radical honesty can be used to combat and to prevent addictions, as well as improve overall quality of life

## conclusion
- immerse yourself fully in the life you've been given
- stop using cheap dopamine to run from what you're trying to escape
