## usage rules
- place a comma before `{and|or|but} <some independent clause>`
- do not join independent clauses with a comma
- A participle at the start of a sentence must refer to the subject (not the object)
    - participle: verb turned into an adjective (e.g. laugh (v)-> laughing (adj))
## composition rules
- 1 paragraph per topic
- for each paragraph, begin with a topic sentence, end with something related
    - e.g. re-state with extra emphasis, state some consequence
- use active voice (i.e. subject -> verb -> object, instead of object <- verb <- subject)
- put statements in positive form (e.g. he was late > he was not on time)
- remove unnecessary words
- don't spam loose sentences, mix in some periodic sentences too
    - loose: main idea first (e.g. `He completed the project last Friday`)
    - periodic: main idea last (e.g. `Last Friday, he completed the project`)
- however, if the sentences are "symmetrical" (e.g. listing different rules, talking about different countries, etc.) then it's better to write them with the same structure
- group related words
- maintain 1 tense in a summary
- put words you want to emphasise at the end or start of the sentence

## form rules
- leave blank line after headings
- don't spell out numbers
- put punctuation after the parentheses, unless the expression within requires it
- for formal quotes: colon and quotation marks, for quotes that appear as an object (e.g. he says ...), just quotation marks will do
- state references either in parentheses or footnotes, not in the middle of the sentence
- format titles with _Italics With Capitalised Initials_
