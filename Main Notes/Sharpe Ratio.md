## definition
> A measure of the investment's risk-adjusted performance, calculated by comparing its return to that of a risk-free asset

## formula
- $Sharpe = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}$
- $R_p$ = average return of portfolio over a period of time
- $R_f$ = average risk-free rate (e.g. rate of US Treasury Securities) over that period of time
- $\sigma_p$ = standard deviation of the portfolio's excess return over that period of time

## intuition
- **reward-to-variability**: measure how much a portfolio's returns are based on risk
- example: someone lucking out of meme stocks may have a high return, but also a low Sharpe => not necessarily a good portfolio
