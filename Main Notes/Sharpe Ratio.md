## definition
> A measure of the investment's risk-adjusted performance, calculated by comparing its return to that of a risk-free asset

## formula
- $Sharpe = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}$
- $R_p$ = return of portfolio
- $R_f$ = risk-free rate (e.g. rate of US Treasury Securities)
- $\sigma_p$ = standard deviation of the portfolio's excess return

## intuition
- measure how much a portfolio's returns are based on risk
- example: someone lucking out of meme stocks may have a high return, but also a high Sharpe => not necessarily a good portfolio
