## misogi
- we live in an age of constant comfort
- problem creep: as we experience fewer problems, we lower our threshold for what we consider to be a problem
- comfort creep: when we get new comforts, we adapt and it becomes the new norm, making older comforts unacceptable
- misogi: overcoming great discomfort to obtain confidence and competence
    - rule 1: do something _really fucking hard_ (50% chance of success, even if you do everything right)
    - rule 2: don't die

## boredom, ideally in nature
- the brain is either focused or unfocused
- killing boredom with technological distractions puts us in focused mode, leading to mental fatigue - like junk food for the mind
- shinrin-yoku: forest bathing, 15 minutes sitting in / walking through nature to improve mental and physical health
- while any time in nature is good, >= 5 hours / month is optimal

## hunger
- in the developed world, we no longer have extended periods where we have to go without food
- we take 12-16 hours to metabolise a meal, after which we enter _autophagy_, where old and weak cells are consumed

## death
- think deeply about your death and acknowledge it
- let go of the anxiety of death and the pettiness of life
