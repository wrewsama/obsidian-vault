## interesting points
- Society has evolved from a disciplinary society to an achievement society
- Instead of being forced to work, people work due to the pressure to achieve
- positive potency: the power to **do**
- negative potency: the power to **not do**
    - this, like Zen meditation, is an active process
    - the converse, hyperactivity, is a passive process
- we are too alive to die, and too dead to live

## my reflection
- I disagree with the premise that "pressure to achieve =>hard work => burnout"
    - "The struggle itself towards the heights is enough to fill a man's heart" - Camus
    - "One who has a why to live can bear with any how" - Nietzsche
    - There is value in the betterment of oneself, and the work done to get it is its own reward
- I like the concept of negative potency
    - modern society, with its constantly improving stream of distractions and cheap dopamine, continually undermines our negative potency
    - this definitely does lead to burnout, as we invest hours of effort into meaningless nonsense
